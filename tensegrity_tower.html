Tensegrity Tower

I have been fascinated with tensegrity ever since I first cracked open my copy of Synergetics by Buckminster Fuller. If you've never encountered his work, and you're courageous I highly recommend this tome. It is, hands down, the most important work of literature that I have ever (partially) read. I'd put it on the same level as the Bhagavad Gita.

I really wanted to explore the possibilities of building tensegrity structures, but I also wanted to play with microcontrollers and learn to solder as I had just gotten my Hakko FX-888D(and I am still in love years later). So, why not combine the two interests? The video in figure 1 shows my first test of the C code which is running the animations.

This was really just to ensure that I was driving the LEDs properly before soldering on the other lengths.

Figure 2 shows the second test. The tower, once built, was to be 4 stories tall, requiring 4 segments of LEDs. I needed to verify that I could run animations across all 4 segments.

Figure 3 is really a combination of the first two tests, just to verify that all my connections were mechanically and electrically stable before building the tower and attaching the LED strips.

Figure 4 is the final product. The code linked above is exactly what you see running in this video. Building the tower and learning how to tie a truker's hitch was 1/2 of the challenge and the fun. Unfortunately I didn't capture any of the knotty business on video.
